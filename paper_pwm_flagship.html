Physics World Models for Computational Imaging: A Universal Physics-Information Law for Recoverability, Carrier Noise, and Operator Mismatch

Chengshuai Yang, Xin Yuan
NextGen PlatformAI C Corp, USA; School of Engineering, Westlake University, China

Abstract

Computational imaging systems routinely fail in practice because the assumed forward model diverges from the true physics, yet no existing framework systematically diagnoses why reconstruction degrades. We introduce Physics World Models (PWM), a universal diagnostic and correction framework where imaging failures stem from three root causes: information deficiency in measurements (Gate 1), insufficient signal-to-noise ratio (Gate 2), or mismatch between assumed and true forward operators (Gate 3). PWM uses an OperatorGraph intermediate representation to encode forward models across diverse imaging modalities into a directed acyclic graph, compiling 64 modalities spanning five physical carriers (photons, electrons, spins, acoustic waves, and particles). Autonomous, deterministic agents diagnose the dominant failure gate and correct the forward model without retraining any reconstruction algorithm. Testing across seven modalities (CASSI, CACTI, SPC, lensless, ptychography, MRI, CT) demonstrates recovery of +0.76 to +48.25 dB of degradation, with a median improvement of +14.5 dB across 26 validated modalities. Correcting the forward model yields larger gains than upgrading the solver -- establishing that a decade of solver-centric progress has overlooked the principal source of imaging failure.

Keywords: Computational imaging, Physics world models, Operator mismatch, Forward model calibration, Triad Law
