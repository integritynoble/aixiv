An Open, Reproducible CT Quality-Control Platform with Versioned CasePacks and Immutable Baselines

Chengshuai Yang
NextGen PlatformAI C Corp
physics.med-ph cs.SE
Submitted
February 2026
Paper ID
aiXiv:2502.006
Abstract

We introduce the Physics World Model CT Quality-Control Platform, an open-source, reproducible software framework for automated CT quality assurance. The platform makes three main contributions: (1) CasePacks -- versioned, declarative workflow descriptors that encode phantom type, metric definitions, tolerance thresholds, and analysis parameters into portable YAML bundles; (2) a four-layer threshold system providing graded pass/marginal/warning/fail classifications aligned with ACR, AAPM TG-233, and site-specific requirements; and (3) immutable CommissioningBundles with cryptographic signing that establish tamper-evident reference baselines for each scanner-protocol pair. The platform validates nine ACR-aligned metrics (CT number accuracy, uniformity, noise, CNR, geometric accuracy, slice thickness, low-contrast detectability, spatial resolution, and artifact assessment) on phantom data with zero inter-run variance and sub-second computation per metric. Statistical process control is implemented via Shewhart charts with Western Electric rules for longitudinal trending. The architecture is extensible to PET/CT and SPECT/CT quality assurance through creation of modality-specific CasePacks, and supports multi-site fleet management through centralized dashboards with per-scanner drill-down.

Keywords: CT quality control, Medical physics, Reproducibility, Statistical process control, Open-source, CasePacks, Phantom analysis
